How It Works: 4 Phases in 12 Weeks

Each phase builds on the last. By Week 12, your business runs on systems — not your memory.

Phase 1

Diagnose

Weeks 1–2

Map where you're stuck. Every task gets a dollar value. You'll see exactly how many hours you're spending on $15/hr work — and which tasks to kill, delegate, or keep.

  • TRAPPED Assessment (founder + company)
  • Time Audit with dollar-value tags
  • Kill List — tasks to eliminate entirely
  • Top 3 delegation targets identified
Phase 2

Delegate

Weeks 3–6

Hand off 3 critical processes with proper systems. Learn to brief, hand off, review, and give feedback — instead of silently rewriting everything yourself.

  • 3 processes with owners and accountability
  • Delegation Brief template for every handoff
  • Feedback framework practiced in real situations
  • Your avoidance pattern identified and addressed
Phase 3

Rhythm

Weeks 7–10

Install a weekly operating cadence so your team runs through systems, not your memory. Kill the status-update meeting. Start real 1:1s. Set decision boundaries.

  • Weekly team meeting redesigned (issues-based)
  • Weekly 1:1s with every direct report
  • Scorecard with 3–5 key metrics
  • Decision boundaries — so your team stops asking permission for everything
Phase 4

Sustain

Weeks 11–12

Stress-test everything. You step away for a full week — no Slack, no email, no calls. We find what holds and fix what doesn't.

  • Week-Off Test — the real proof of delivery
  • Before/after Time Audit comparison
  • Gap diagnosis: systems, people, or founder dependency
  • Clarity on your next strategic priorities

Common Questions

What if my team isn't capable enough to take on more?

This is the most common fear — and usually wrong. Most teams underperform because they've never been given clear briefs, quality bars, or feedback. The Delegation Brief and Feedback Framework fix this. In cases where there is a genuine capability gap, we flag it early (Phase 1) and build a plan around it.

I've tried delegating before and it didn't work. Why is this different?

Most founders delegate by saying "just handle this" and hoping for the best. Then they're disappointed and take it back. This program installs a repeatable system: structured briefs, scheduled check-ins, and a feedback framework so your team knows exactly what "good" looks like. You'll also identify your specific avoidance pattern — whether you're a Silent Rewriter, Vague Briefer, or Perfectionist Blocker — so you can catch yourself before you fall back into old habits.

How much time do I need between sessions?

Plan for 2–3 hours/week for exercises and implementation. This isn't homework for homework's sake — it's actual work on your business (building briefs, running 1:1s, redesigning your team meeting). Most founders say the exercises save them more time than they take within the first few weeks.

What happens during the free diagnostic call?

You'll take the TRAPPED Assessment — a 7-dimension diagnostic that maps where you're bottlenecked as a leader and where your company is stuck organizationally. You'll leave with your top 2–3 constraints identified and clarity on what to fix first. There's no pitch unless you ask. The call is valuable on its own.

What does the guarantee mean exactly?

If you complete the exercises and show up to every call, and you haven't recovered 10+ hours/week of low-value work by Week 12, we keep working together for up to 4 additional weeks at no extra cost. This isn't a money-back offer — it's a commitment to the outcome.

Do you work with my team directly?

No. You're coached on how to introduce changes to your team — how to run the meta-conversation about feedback, how to roll out the new meeting format, how to set decision boundaries. Your team experiences better management from you, not coaching from an outsider.
